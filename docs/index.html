By Yating Wang

The data from the New York City Health Department reveals a concerning prevalence of heavy metal contamination in consumer goods across multiple categories. In 2023. Food-Spice topped the list with 140 detected cases. Besides food-spice with unspecified origin, most came from Bangladesh, Guyana, India, and Pakistan. Dietary supplements, medications, and remedies followed closely with 115 cases with majority manufactured in China and Hong Kong. The department found 74 cases of tableware and pottery contaminated by heavy metal. Cosmetics and children's products, though fewer in number (14 and 11 cases respectively), raised alarms due to their direct contact with skin or ingestion risks.

Notably, the lack of information on manufacturing countries, which are labeled within “UNKNOWN”, highlights gaps in supply chain oversight, leaving consumers vulnerable to invisible dangers.
